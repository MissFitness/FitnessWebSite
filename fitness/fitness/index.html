USEFUL

Several foods we should eat every day:

Pure proteins

They help speed up your metabolism and encourage your body to burn more bad fat. Turkey, chicken breasts, pork or beef fillet provide you with muscle-building proteins without contributing to the accumulation of unwanted fat. Plug the meat and eggs into the menu, but do not overdo it.

"Winter" vegetables

They are low in calories and high in fiber and essential nutrients. Carifiol, broccoli, cabbage, Brussels sprouts and beans are part of the group of cruciferous vegetables. Studies have shown that these vegetables can help prevent cancer by reducing oxidative stress and stimulating enzymes in the body that fight carcinogenic substances.

Fruits

These are very important when it comes to eating a balanced diet. Blueberries, blackberries, raspberries, etc. are an incredibly powerful source of antioxidants that help fight free radicals, slow down aging and reduce the risk of cancer. Fruits are always healthy and refreshing snacks and are especially good choices when you want something sweet, counsel nutritionists.

Bananas

Bananas are one of the easiest foods you need to include regularly in your diet because they are very versatile and comfortable. Eat a fruit before a sport or while you run for work. Studies have shown that bananas have a high potassium content that can reduce the risk of high blood pressure and stroke.

Health & Fitness

Train with partner

The benefits of partner training are many, but it is important to note that not everyone is suitable for one. When choosing a fitness partner, find someone who shares your fitness goals in the long run - this will ensure that you and your two are always committed and motivated for a good workout. By common goals, we do not mean either of you want to lose weight, or both to face a rise in muscle mass but just someone who really wants to train and is serious in their intentions - if that is not the case, you risk not getting the emotional support and motivation you seek.

Tips for success

Finding the right partner with whom to share your workouts is the first step, but to make your partnership work and succeed, follow these few tips:

- Make your goals clear and set up a schedule.

- Every month mark your development.

- Diversify your workouts and make them fun.
